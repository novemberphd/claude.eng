The Three Key Tenses

Master these three tenses and handle 90% of everyday English conversations

Past Simple
Completed actions in the past
Subject + V2 (past form)
  • I walked to school yesterday.
  • She studied English last year.
  • They went to the cinema on Sunday.
  • He didn't watch TV last night.

Signal Words

yesterday last week ago in 2020 then
Present Simple
Habits, routines, and general truths
Subject + V1 (base / +s for he/she/it)
  • I wake up at 7 am every day.
  • She speaks three languages.
  • The sun rises in the east.
  • He doesn't eat meat.

Signal Words

always usually every day never often
Future Simple
Decisions, predictions, and promises
Subject + will + V1 (base form)
  • I will call you tomorrow.
  • She will graduate next year.
  • It will rain in the afternoon.
  • They won't be late, I promise.

Signal Words

tomorrow next week soon in 2030 someday
Expert Advice

Tips to Learn Faster

Proven strategies from language learning experts to accelerate your progress

01
Practice 20 Minutes Daily
Consistency beats intensity. 20 minutes every day is far more effective than 3 hours once a week. Your brain consolidates language during sleep — daily practice maximizes this.
02
Think in English
Stop translating in your head. When you see an apple, think "apple" — not your native word. This builds direct connections between thoughts and English words.
03
Learn Words in Context
Don't memorize lists of isolated words. Learn each new word in a sentence. Context gives meaning and makes words 3x easier to remember long-term.
04
Watch English Media
Movies, series, YouTube — immerse yourself in real English. Start with subtitles in your native language, then switch to English subtitles, then none at all.
05
Don't Fear Mistakes
Every mistake is a lesson. Native speakers respect effort, not perfection. The fastest learners make the most mistakes — because they practice the most.
06
Use Spaced Repetition
Review vocabulary at increasing intervals: 1 day, 3 days, 1 week, 1 month. This method moves words from short-term to long-term memory efficiently.
